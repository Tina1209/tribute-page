Christian Dior

A man who created unsurpassed sophisticated fashion for women

Here's a time line of Christian Dior life:

  • 1905 - Christian Dior is born on January 21 in Granville, France.
  • 1906 - Dior's family moves to Paris, where he spends his childhood surrounded by art and culture.
  • 1918 - Dior's mother passes away, leaving a profound impact on his life and artistic sensibilities.
  • 1928 - Dior's family business fails, prompting him to pursue a career in art.
  • 1928-1935 - Dior studies political science before eventually pursuing his passion for art, enrolling at the Ã‰cole des Sciences Politiques.
  • 1935-1938 - Dior opens an art gallery with a friend, displaying the works of artists such as Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque.
  • 1938 - Dior secures a job as a design assistant for fashion designer Robert Piguet, marking the beginning of his fashion career.
  • 1938-1940 - Dior serves in the French military during World War II, but is demobilized due to health issues.
  • 1941-1946 - Dior works for the fashion house of Lucien Lelong, designing dresses for the wives of Nazi officers and French collaborators.
  • 1946 - Dior founds his own fashion house, Christian Dior S.A., with financial backing from textile magnate Marcel Boussac.
  • 1947 - On February 12, Dior unveils his groundbreaking "New Look" collection, redefining post-war fashion with its feminine silhouette.
  • 1947 - Dior launches his first perfume, "Miss Dior," named after his sister Catherine Dior, a member of the French Resistance.
  • 1949 - Dior expands internationally, opening a boutique in New York City, followed by locations in London and Buenos Aires.
  • 1950 - The House of Dior introduces its first couture eyewear collection, further expanding its product range.
  • 1952 - Dior launches his iconic fragrance, "Diorissimo," which becomes a symbol of elegance and luxury.
  • 1954 - Dior publishes his autobiography, "Dior by Dior," offering insights into his creative process and the evolution of his fashion empire.
  • 1955 - Dior expands into ready-to-wear fashion with the launch of his "Christian Dior-Caron" line.
  • 1957 - Tragically, Christian Dior passes away on October 24 from a heart attack while on vacation in Italy, leaving behind a fashion empire at the age of 52.
